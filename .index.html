🔍 Bank Robbery Detective Game

An ESL Learning Adventure

Based on an activity from Ready to Write by Karen Blanchard and Christine Root.
Adapted for interactive learning by Oliver Mann with Claude Sonnet 4.5.

📰 Background: Computer Crime Hits Local Bank

National City Bank and Trust Company is the largest bank in the city, holding assets in the billions of dollars. In 1960, the bank computerized its operations and had considered itself fortunate for avoiding any issues with computer crime—until last week.

Last Wednesday, bank accountants discovered that a total of $400,000 was missing from several different accounts. So far, it is unknown where the funds were transferred. A police investigation has led to three possible suspects. These three individuals had easy access to the computer system that transferred the funds out of the bank.

🎭 Your Role

You are a detective investigating this robbery. Three suspects have been identified, each with access to the bank's computer system. You must question each suspect carefully. Each suspect will only confess if your questions and conversation meet their language proficiency level.

📋 Game Rules

How the AI Responds:

  • Uses vocabulary and grammar appropriate for each suspect's level
  • If your question formation is wrong: "Do you have a question for me?"
  • If your verb tense is wrong: "When are you asking me about?"
  • Tracks your progress with encouraging feedback after each successful exchange

Progress Tracking:

  • Exchange 1/5: "Good start! 👍"
  • Exchange 2/5: "Nice work! 😊"
  • Exchange 3/5: "Well done! 🌟"
  • Exchange 4/5: "Excellent! 🎯"
  • Exchange 5/5: "Outstanding! You can now try for a confession. 🏆"

Getting a Confession:

  • After 5 successful exchanges at the correct level, you can accuse the suspect
  • The suspect will confess with a random, humorous, or tragic motive
  • If you try too early: "You can't talk to me that way!" (maintains innocence)

🎯 How to Play

  1. Choose a suspect to interview (start with A2 for easier practice)
  2. Tell the AI: "I am ready to interview [Suspect Name] ([Level])"
  3. Ask questions appropriate to their language level
  4. Pay attention to grammar and verb tenses
  5. Complete 5 successful exchanges
  6. Make your accusation and get the confession!

Example prompt to start:

"I am ready to interview Richard Allen (B1 level). Please act as my ESL teacher roleplaying as Richard Allen."
